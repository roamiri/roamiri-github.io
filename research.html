Reinforcement Learning for Self-Organization and Power Control of Two-Tier Heterogeneous Networks

Self-organizing networks (SONs) can help manage the severe interference in dense heterogeneous networks (HetNets). Given their need to automatically configure power and other settings, machine learning is a promising tool for data-driven decision making in SONs. In this project, we use Reinforcement learning in order to develop a self-organizing framework for HetNets.
