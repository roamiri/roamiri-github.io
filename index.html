About

I am a PhD candidate at the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at Boise State University. My research is supervised by Dr. Hani Mehrpouyan. Currently, I am visiting the Wireless Networking and Communications Group (WNCG) at University of Texas at Austin under the supervision of Professor Jeffrey Andrews. My research interst is developing self-organizing methods in wireless networks using the reinforcement learning approaches. These methods are used to manage resources of Heterogeneous networks (HetNets), and to manage topology of dense wireless networks. Another passion of mine is developing platforms to accelerate wireless communication processes via parallel processing. From computation tools perspective, I have worked with CUDA and OpenMP platforms. I also love to write platforms of my own such as Dense Network Simulator that is currently being developed.
